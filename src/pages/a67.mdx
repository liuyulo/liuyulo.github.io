<details class="example" open>
<summary>(PMI) wts $4^n-1$ is divisible by $3$ for any integer $n>0$</summary>
let $P(n)$ be the proposition $4^n-1$ is divisible by $3$
- we use PMI to prove that $P(n)$ is true for every integer $n>0$
- basis step
  - $P(0)$ is the statement $4^0-1$ is divisible by $3$
  - this is true because $4^0-1=1-1=0$ which is divisible by $3$
- induction step
  - for IH, let $k>0$ be an arbitrary integer and assume that $4^k-1$ is divisible by $3$
  - wts $4^{k+1}-1$ is divisible by $3$
$$
\begin{align*}
\because\,&4^k-1\textsf{ is divisible by }3\tag{\sf IH}\\
\therefore\,&\textsf{by def, there is an integer }m\textsf{ st }4^k-1=3m\tag{\sf PA1}\\
  \therefore\,&4^{k+1}-1=\underline{4\cdot4^k}-1=\underline{3\cdot4^k+\color{red}4^k}{\color{red}-1}=3\cdot4^k+{\color{red}3m}=3(4^k+m)\\
  \because\,&k\textsf{ and }m\textsf{ are integers, so is }4^k+m\\
  \therefore\,&\textsf{by def, }4^{k+1}-1=3(4^k+m)\textsf{ is divisible by }3\textsf{ as wanted}\\
\end{align*}
$$
- $\therefore$ by PMI $4^n-1$ is divisible by $3$ for any integer $n>0$
</details>

<details class="example">
<summary>bonus wts $4^n-1$ is divisible by $3$</summary>
let any $n\in\mathbb N$, then consider $(2^n-1)(2^n)(2^n+1)$
- $\because$ its product of 3 consecutive integers
- $\therefore$ one of $2^n-1$, $2^n$, $2^n+1$ must be divisible by $3$
- $\because 2^n$ only has $2$ as its factors
- $\therefore 2^n$ is not divisible by $3$
- $\therefore 2^n-1$ or $2^n+1$ is divisible by $3$
- $\therefore 4^n-1=(2^n-1)(2^n+1)$ is divisible by $3\quad\blacksquare$
</details>

- let $P(n)$ be $S_n=3-1/3^n<3$
- we use PSI to prove that $P(n)$ is true for any integer $n>0$
- basis step:
  - $P(1)$ is the statement $S_1=3-1/3<3$
  - this is true because $S_1=2+2/3=(2+1)+(-1+2/3)=3-1/3<3$
- induction step:
  - for IH, let $k>0$ be an arbitrary integer and assume that $S_k=3-1/3^k<3$
  - wts $S_{k+1}=3-1/3^{k+1}<3$
  $$
  \begin{align*}
    S_{k+1}&={\color{red}2+\frac23+\frac29+\cdots+\frac2{3^k}}+\frac2{3^{k+1}}\\
    &={\color{red}S_k}+\frac2{3^{k+1}}\tag{\sf def of $S_k$}\\
    &={\color{red}3-\frac1{3^k}}+\frac2{3^{k+1}}\tag{\sf IH}\\
    &=3-\frac3{3\cdot 3^k}+\frac2{3^{k+1}}=3-\frac3{3^{k+1}}+\frac2{3^{k+1}}\\
    &=3-\frac1{3^{k+1}}\\
  \end{align*}
  $$
  $$
  \begin{align*}
    \because\,&k>0\\
    \therefore\,&\frac1{3^{k+1}}>0\\
    \therefore\,&S_{k+1}=3-\frac1{3^k+1}<3\textsf{ as wanted}
  \end{align*}
  $$
- $\therefore$ by PMI $\displaystyle S_n=\sum_{i=0}^n\frac2{3^i}<3$ for any nonnegative integer $n\quad\blacksquare$
